Seacast is Moving!

- Date Posted: 04/24/2018

After 6 years of operation and development at Oregon State University, Seacast.org is shutting down! Seacast.org has been funded by a grant provided by Sea Grant which is scheduled to end in the summer of 2018. The development team is pleased and happy with the findings produced by Seacast.org and value having the site as a service to the community.

Because of this, the Seacast team has teamed up with our friends at the Northwest Association of Networked Ocean Observing Systems (NANOOS) to continue hosting Seacast.org. The new site has a new look and feel and has all the same features and more including: data at the cursor, more tidal locations, separated wind and swell wave heights and bathymetric depth contours. You can visit the site at: http://nvs.nanoos.org/Seacast.

If you have any questions or comments with the new site or the old site, please use the feedback forms on Seacast.org!
